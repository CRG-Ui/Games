Momentum: Mass in Motion

The Core Formula

Momentum is simply the product of an object's mass and its velocity. This elegant formula is the key to everything.

p = m × v

Momentum (p)

p

The quantity of motion an object has.

kg⋅m/s

Mass (m)

The amount of matter in an object.

kg

Velocity (v)

v

The speed of an object in a direction.

m/s

Momentum in Action: A Comparison

Momentum depends on both mass and velocity. A small, fast object can have comparable momentum to a large, slow one. This chart shows the momentum of various objects from the examples.

The Momentum Paradox: Velocity Dominance

It's counterintuitive, but a small object moving fast can have *significantly* more momentum than a large object moving slowly. This demonstrates the powerful multiplication effect of Velocity ($v$).
